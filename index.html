QA Status Classification Guide

How GatiQA decides Exception vs Manual Review vs Conforms — and what each status means for your QA review.

The Core Principle
GatiQA can only raise an Exception when it has (1) the document uploaded AND (2) the document fails an objective, measurable test. If either condition is not met, the result is Manual Review. This mirrors IIA Standard 2330 — a finding cannot be raised without documented evidence.
⚠
Exception
Automated deficiency detected — action required
Definition: GatiQA uploaded the document, analysed its content, and the evidence objectively fails a measurable IIA/QA standard.
What triggers it
  • Duplicate rows detected in RCM
  • APM scope exclusion has no rationale
  • Prior year finding not in current APM
  • Control description under 100 chars / <4/6 5W1H
  • Report missing completion dates for findings
  • TOD steps copy-pasted across controls
  • Walkthrough MoM missing attendees / date
What to do
  • Read the Finding and Recommendation columns
  • Open the annotated source document (click the row)
  • Remediate the identified deficiency
  • Upload the corrected document
  • Re-run QA cycle to verify it passes
⚠ All Exceptions must be remediated or formally accepted (with documented rationale) before the QA review can be closed.
⤴
Manual Review
QA reviewer action needed — two distinct reasons
Definition: GatiQA cannot auto-conclude this checkpoint. This happens for exactly two reasons — shown below. It is not the same as an Exception.
Reason A — Document Not Uploaded
The required evidence document was not uploaded to this engagement. GatiQA cannot analyse what it cannot see. This is not a finding — it is a data gap.
What to do: Upload the missing document (see the Source Doc column), then click ⟳ Re-run QA.
Identified by: "not uploaded" in the Finding text
Reason B — Professional Judgement Required
The check requires a QA reviewer to read the content and apply professional judgement. GatiQA has analysed what it can but cannot make a binary Pass/Fail without human assessment.
What to do: Review the evidence, make your own assessment, and document your conclusion in the remarks field.
Examples: CP09 (risk taxonomy), CP18 (PII in screenshots), CP28 (MAP–root cause coherence)
⤴ Manual Review items must all be cleared (either passed or escalated to Exception) before the engagement QA score is finalised.
✓
Pass
Standard met — no action required
Definition: GatiQA analysed the uploaded document and the evidence objectively meets the required IIA/QA standard for this checkpoint. The reviewer may still open the evidence to read it, but no corrective action is needed.
Examples: APM has all 6 required sections · RCM has no duplicate rows and all descriptions pass 5W1H · Audit report has root cause per finding · Sample sizes match Sampling Matrix
IIA Standards Basis
IIA Standard 2200
Engagement Planning
Basis for CP01–CP04 checks. Requires documented objectives, scope, timing, and risk assessment before fieldwork.
IIA Standard 2330
Documenting Information
Auditors must document sufficient, reliable, relevant evidence. Undocumented = Manual Review. Documented but deficient = Exception.
IIA Standard 2410
Criteria for Communicating
Basis for CP23–CP29. Reports must include objectives, scope, conclusions, recommendations, and management responses.
📌 Summary for Management
Exception = GatiQA found the document and the document fails an objective, measurable IIA standard. These are confirmed deficiencies.
Manual Review = Either the required document is missing (upload it and re-run), or the check requires a human reviewer's professional judgement to conclude.
Pass = GatiQA confirmed the evidence meets the standard. Reviewer may validate but no action is required.

The QA Score excludes Manual Review items from the denominator — it reflects only what has been conclusively assessed. Once all Manual Reviews are cleared, the score updates to reflect the full engagement.
